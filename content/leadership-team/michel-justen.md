Michel co-founded EA UW in Fall 2020 after hearing a podcast episode—as one does. Alongside his twin, Lenni, and a team of early organizers, he helped shape the group into a community of standout people committed to leaving the world better than they found it. He graduated from UW-Madison in 2022 with degrees in Neurobiology and Psychology and has since moved to San Francisco to work at the intersection of AI safety and infrastructure. After running high-level events for the Centre for Effective Altruism and researching international benefit-sharing at Pivotal Research, he now serves as AI Grants Lead at the Tarbell Center for AI Journalism, supporting journalism that helps society navigate the emergence of advanced AI.
