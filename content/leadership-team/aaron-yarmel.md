Aaron Yarmel (PhD, MSc, MA) is the Associate Director of the Center for Ethics and Human Values at The Ohio State University. At CEHV, he oversees program operations and leads university-wide initiatives on dialogue facilitation, civil discourse, and the ETHOS Fellows program. His academic research explores two-level utilitarianism, social change, and the philosophy of education. He earned his PhD in Philosophy from UW–Madison and served as the founding director of Madison Public Philosophy, an organization dedicated to bringing philosophical inquiry to children and the broader public. Aaron has been an active member of the Effective Altruism movement since 2015. He has contributed through his research, as a guest speaker and workshop presenter, and as an EAGx conference organizer. Outside of his work at OSU, he continues to offer philosophical counseling and dialogue facilitation training through his private consulting practice.
