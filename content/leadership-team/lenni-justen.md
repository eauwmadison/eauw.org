Lenni co-founded EA UW–Madison together with his brother in 2020 when he was a sophomore. Together, with a wonderful team of dedicated people, he helped create a community of people in Madison inspired to do good in the world. Lenni graduated from UW–Madison in 2022 with a B.S. in Physics and Political Science. Currently, Lenni is a PhD Candidate at MIT and the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard, where he is advised by Pardis Sabeti and Kevin Esvelt. His work is at the cutting edge of biosecurity, focusing on detecting emerging pathogens through wastewater metagenomics and suppressing transmission in built environments. Beyond his doctoral research, Lenni serves as a Research Manager at ERA Cambridge, focusing on the intersection of AI and biology. His previous experience includes a fellowship for ending bioweapons at the Council on Strategic Risks and work with the U.S. Department of State on the bioeconomy.
